Automating DailyPay

Designing for the launch of DailyPay’s self-service product.

Role

Product Design Generalist
  • Challenges
  • Key Results
  • Takeaways
  • Next Steps

Overview

The labor market has seen challenges in competing for hourly workers in businesses of all sizes. DailyPay offers an earned wage access solution as a benefit for employees of larger corporations, but it’s the companies with fewer employees that lack the resources who find themselves at a disadvantage. As part of this initiative, we began to launch the self-service portal — a web experience designed to lay the path to the sign up flow by educating product value, illustrating ease of implementation and employee benefits.

The self-service model may be new to DailyPay, but most of us have interacted with the e-commerce concept in one form or another.

How might we bring awareness of the full product offerings in a swift checkout manner?

Workshops to activate team alignment

Since the first phase of this pilot launch needed a quick turnaround, we grouped discovery and research into a 2-day workshop with the stakeholders and the working team.

What we extracted

Owners of small businesses of less than 500 are not only competing with larger organizations for talented workers but also retaining them once they’re onboard.

User Statement

I need a way to recruit, reward and retain employees that isn’t just training them for larger competitors.

We had a quick look at other Saas platforms to observe and challenge our thinking.

The landscape

There was an obvious pattern: brevity in messaging (often in bullet lists) and descriptive call-to-actions that hint at urgency.

Telling stories through lo-fi's

Two wireframe variations with very different approaches: a long scrolling version provides more storytelling and a postcard approach that keeps the focus contained.

Based on an initial brainstorming session, I created these as a starting point and to give some direction on content and design. They helped us get initial stakeholder feedback and as we needed to move quickly into high-fidelity design.

A cohesive step forward

The stakeholders had “Amazon’s Checkout Process” of streamlining the purchase experience in mind — naturally, the postcard approach was the clear winner. At this point, the content was beginning to shape in parallel to the design iterations.

Connecting the dots

With our layout approach defined, we moved into building out the buyer’s journey with a focus on how to move the user from learning, evaluating, and selecting DailyPay to enrollment.

While content was making its round between stakeholder feedback, exploration on its presentation was underway.

Option 1: Expansion Panels

The three steps of the journey are shown in collapsed panels to display summary information with more content revealed upon expansion. It aimed to lead with a snippet of the narrative that would prompt the interaction to expand the panels.

Option 2: Folder Tabs

Functioning tabs with descriptive headers served as navigational cues that allowed easy jumps between a number of equally important views.

The Good

Each tab acts as a sign post and minimizes other distractions for the user.

Content organization is connected: the same level of importance is placed between all the items in the tab bar.

A decision was made: the folder tabs approach — for its clear, directional cues that offer enough scalability for the next immediate phases.
